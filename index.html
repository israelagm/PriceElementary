  • About Sarah J. Price

    Sarah J. Price was born in Clintonville in Bourbon County, Kentucky, on March 8, 1906.  She attended schools in Paris, Kentucky, the county seat of Bourbon County.  After high school, she studied at what is now Kentucky State University for two years, then began her teaching career in Bourbon County.  In 1928 she moved to the schools in Harlan, Kentucky.  In her first year there, Mrs. Price taught all eight grades in one school.  In 1930 she left Harlan to teach in Fayette County.

    She became a teacher at Newburg School in 1947 and taught until she was named the school’s principal in 1959.  Mrs. Price continued her education at the University of Louisville, receiving her bachelor’s degree in 1951.  She earned a master’s degree from Indiana University in 1953.

    Sarah J. Price was the principal at Newburg School for nine years until her death in 1968, the year before Price Elementary School opened.  Price Elementary opened in 1969 in the former county district, also named the Jefferson County Public Schools.  That district decided to honor the late Mrs. Price by naming our then-new school for her.

Parent Info

Attendance

Regular attendance is very important for the success of a student. Students are allowed inside the building after 8:30 a.m. and classes start promptly at 9:05 a.m Students arriving after 9:05 a.m. have to be checked in by an adult at the front office. We encourage students to attend school even if they are tardy.

Dress Code

Uniforms are required for all students beginning on the first day of school. The approved uniform attire includes:

  • Collared shirts or turtlenecks in any solid color. (Sweaters, sweater vests, or sweatshirts may be worn over.)
  • Uniform style pants, skorts, shorts, or jumpers in khaki, navy, or black only.
  • Closed-toe and closed-heal shoes are required.
  • Not allowed: Biker shorts, tank tops, or tops with innapropriate messages. Roller-skate style tennis shoes.

SBDM

The School-Based Decision Making Council is a panel of faculty members and parents who meet once a month. The SBDM Council shall have the responsibility to set school policy in the areas specified by the law, to provide an environment to enhance students' achievement, and to help the school meet its educational goals.

Code of Conduct

Every student, parent/guardian, and school staff member receives a copy of the code and receives instructions on how to use it. English as a Second Language

English Language Learners (ELLs) are a diverse group of students, with different language, academic, and social-emotional needs. Students who speak the same language or have similar levels of language proficiency may need very different kinds of support to succeed in the classroom. Our ESL teachers work collaboratively with classroom teachers to help students with limited English proficiency. ESL teachers use specially designed methods that help English language learners develop English language and literacy skills while they study required subject matter for their grade levels. Positive Behavior Intervention and Supports

Positive Behavior Intervention and Supports (PBIS) is an evidence-based, proactive system approach to establishing school wide interventions and supports needed for students to achieve success. The purpose of PBIS is to establish a climate at Price Elementary in which appropriate behavior is the usual.

Students receive individual and class P.R.I.D.E. bucks for positive behavior in common areas of the school building. The acronym P.R.I.D.E. stands for Positive attitude, Respect for all, Integrity, Do your best and Effort towards learning. Each week a drawing is held and students from each grade level are given a chance to earn prizes from the PBIS cart. Class incentives are determined by the classroom teacher and the interests of the students.

In addition to P.R.I.D.E. bucks, students receive conduct points in all areas of the school building every day for their behavior. Each nine weeks (at the end of the grading period), an activity is held for students who have a met the number of conduct points required. The goal is for all students to attend and have positive behavior in the school building.

Primary School Program

Kindergarten

In Kindergarten we use strategies that we have learned from the Bellarmine Literacy Project to teach reading and writing related to all content areas. We focus on Phonemic Awareness, Phonics, Vocabulary, Comprehension, and Fluency. We use small guided reading groups to meet students where they are in their academic understandings and we move them along, meeting the Common Core Standards. We incorporate a specific time designated for learning the process of writing while incorporating both science and social studies.

In math, we use a math workshop structure where students are met with in groups to focus on their needs at their level of understanding. Students move to centers focused on differentiated work and games at their levels.

We also incorporate visual arts and character education to enhance their learning.

At Price Elementary School, Kindergarten is a fun and purposeful place for students to begin their elementary school experience.

The Visual Arts Program at Price Elementary provides students with the opportunity to participate in an enhanced learning environment that allows them make community and global connections and learn advanced techniques in drawing, painting, sculpting, and pottery. Local, renowned artists and community organizations partner with our teaching artists and school leaders to offer our students unique educational experiences that enable them to become lifelong learners and productive members of society.
